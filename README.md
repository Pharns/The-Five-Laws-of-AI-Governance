# The Five Laws of AI Governance

**Author:** Pharns Genece
**Version:** 1.0
**Published:** 2026

*Also referred to as **The 5 Laws of AI Governance**.*

---

The **Five Laws of AI Governance** establish the minimum structural conditions required to independently govern an autonomous system — to measure not what a system is *capable* of, but whether it stayed within what it was *authorized* to do, at the moment it acted.

Left ungoverned, an autonomous system collapses four distinct roles into a single actor: it becomes the **judge, witness, owner, and executioner** of its own conduct. The Five Laws strip each of those roles from the governed system and assign it to something external and separable — and one law fixes the *timing*: governance must bind **before** the act, not after.

## The Five Laws

1. **Independent Measure** — A system may not produce the measure of its own conduct.
2. **Binding at Dispatch** — A system may not be governed only in retrospect.
3. **External Witness** — A system may not be the custodian of the record of its own conduct.
4. **Separated Authority** — No party may certify itself.
5. **Revocable Authority** — A system may not outlive its off-switch.

## The Spine

> A system is never the **judge, witness, owner, or executioner** of its own governance — and governance binds **before** the act, not after.

## Scope

This is a **measurement standard for conformance to an authorization** — not a standard for the *content* of the authorization. A system can be perfectly governed into doing something reckless if the grant itself was reckless. The Laws are **necessary, not sufficient, for safety.** Do not mistake *compliant* for *safe*.
